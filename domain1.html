Domain 1: General Security Concepts (12%)

This domain covers foundational security concepts, including the CIA triad, risk management, security controls, and key regulations and standards.

Key Topics

  • CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
  • Security Controls (Preventive, Detective, Corrective, Deterrent)
  • Risk Management Processes
  • Applicable Regulations and Standards (ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR)
  • Security Policies and Procedures

Expanded Explanations

  • CIA Triad: The foundation of information security.
    • Confidentiality: Protecting information from unauthorized access.
      Example: Using encryption for sensitive emails.
    • Encryption
    • Access Controls with IAAA and physical security
    • Integrity: Ensuring data is accurate and unaltered.
      Example: File checksums or digital signatures.
    • Availability: Ensuring resources are accessible when needed.
      Example: Redundant servers to prevent downtime.
  • Security Controls: Measures to reduce risk.
    • Preventive: Firewalls, access controls
    • Detective: IDS, security audits
    • Corrective: Backups, patching
    • Deterrent: Warning signs, security training
  • Risk Management: Identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks.
    Best Practice: Perform regular risk assessments and update controls as needed.
  • Regulations & Standards: Frameworks for compliance and best practices.
    Example: GDPR for privacy, ISO 27001 for ISMS, NIST for federal systems.
  • Policies & Procedures: Written rules and guidelines for security.
    Example: Acceptable Use Policy, Incident Response Plan.

Real-World Scenario

Scenario: A hospital uses encryption (confidentiality), digital signatures (integrity), and backup generators (availability) to protect patient data and ensure operations during a power outage.

Practice Questions

  1. Which part of the CIA triad is impacted if a server crash makes a website unavailable?
    Answer: Availability
  2. What type of control is a security camera?
    Answer: Detective (and Deterrent)
  3. Which regulation focuses on data privacy for EU citizens?
    Answer: GDPR
