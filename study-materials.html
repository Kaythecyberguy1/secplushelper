Domain 1: General Security Concepts (12%)

Key Topics:

  • Compare and contrast various types of security controls
  • Explain the importance of applicable regulations, standards, or frameworks
  • Explain the importance of policies to organizational security
  • Summarize risk management processes and concepts

Study Notes:

Security Controls
  • Preventive controls: Stop incidents from occurring
    • Firewalls, encryption, access controls
    • Security policies and procedures
    • Physical security measures
  • Detective controls: Identify incidents after they occur
    • Intrusion detection systems (IDS)
    • Log monitoring and analysis
    • Security audits and assessments
  • Corrective controls: Restore systems after incidents
    • Backup and recovery procedures
    • Incident response plans
    • Patch management systems
  • Deterrent controls: Discourage potential attackers
    • Warning banners and messages
    • Security awareness training
    • Visible security measures
Risk Management
  • Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact
    • Threat: Potential danger to assets
    • Vulnerability: Weakness that can be exploited
    • Impact: Consequences of a successful attack
  • Risk assessment: Identify, analyze, and evaluate risks
    • Qualitative vs. quantitative assessment
    • Asset valuation and classification
    • Threat modeling and analysis
  • Risk treatment: Accept, transfer, mitigate, or avoid
    • Risk acceptance: Document and monitor
    • Risk transfer: Insurance, outsourcing
    • Risk mitigation: Implement controls
    • Risk avoidance: Eliminate the risk
Key Regulations and Standards
  • ISO 27001: Information security management
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework
  • GDPR: Data protection and privacy
  • PCI DSS: Payment card security
  • HIPAA: Healthcare information security

Additional Resources:

Recommended Reading
  • NIST Special Publication 800-53
  • ISO 27001/27002 Standards
  • COBIT Framework
Practice Questions
  • Security control types and examples
  • Risk management scenarios
  • Regulatory compliance requirements

Domain 2: Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations (22%)

Key Topics:

  • Compare and contrast common threat actors and motivations
  • Explain common threat vectors and attack surfaces
  • Explain various types of vulnerabilities
  • Given a scenario, analyze indicators of malicious activity

Study Notes:

Threat Actors
  • Nation-state actors: Government-sponsored attacks
    • Advanced persistent threats (APTs)
    • Cyber espionage operations
    • Critical infrastructure targeting
  • Hacktivists: Attack for political or social causes
    • Website defacement
    • DDoS attacks
    • Information leaks
  • Organized crime: Attack for financial gain
    • Ransomware operations
    • Credit card fraud
    • Identity theft
  • Script kiddies: Use existing tools without deep knowledge
    • Automated attack tools
    • Common vulnerability exploitation
    • Limited technical skills
Common Attack Vectors
  • Email phishing and spear phishing
    • Social engineering techniques
    • Malicious attachments
    • URL manipulation
  • Malware and ransomware
    • Types of malware (viruses, worms, trojans)
    • Ransomware encryption methods
    • Command and control (C2) infrastructure
  • Social engineering attacks
    • Pretexting and impersonation
    • Baiting and quid pro quo
    • Tailgating and piggybacking
  • Zero-day exploits
    • Vulnerability discovery
    • Exploit development
    • Patch management challenges
Vulnerability Management
  • Vulnerability scanning tools and techniques
  • Patch management process
  • Configuration management
  • Vulnerability assessment vs. penetration testing

Additional Resources:

Recommended Reading
  • MITRE ATT&CK Framework
  • OWASP Top 10
  • NIST Vulnerability Database
Practice Questions
  • Threat actor identification
  • Attack vector analysis
  • Vulnerability assessment scenarios
